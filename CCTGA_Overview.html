Should my patient with Congenitally-corrected transposition of the great arteries (CC-TGA, L-TGA) participate in organized sports?

Need to perform:

  • Clinical assessment
  • ECG
  • Imaging of ventricular function
  • Exercise testing

"It is recommended that before participation in competitive sports, all CCTGA athletes should undergo evaluation that includes clinical assessment, ECG, imaging assessment of ventricular function, and exercise testing (Class I; Level of Evidence B)."

⬋        ⬋         ⬊        ⬊

Asymptomatic, no abnormalities on evaluation

"Asymptomatic athletes with CCTGA and without abnormalities on clinical evaluation may be considered for participation in moderate- to high-intensity competitive sports (classes II and IIIB or IIIC) (Class IIb; Level of Evidence C)."

May consider II, IIIB, IIIC sports.

Without arrhythmias, ventricular dysfunction, exercise intolerance, or exercise-induced ischemia

"Athletes with CCTGA and without clinically significant arrhythmias, ventricular dysfunction, exercise intolerance, or exercise-induced ischemia may be considered for participation in low- and moderate-intensity competitive sports (class IA and IB) (Class IIb; Level of Evidence C)."

May consider Class IA and IB sports.

Arrhythmias, severe ventricular dysfunction

"Participation in competitive sports in those CCTGA athletes with a history of clinically significant arrhythmias or severe ventricular dysfunction may be considered on an individual basis based on clinical stability (Class IIb; Level of Evidence C)."

Consider on an individual basis

Severe systemic RV dysfunction, severe RVOTO, recurrent arrhythmias

"Athletes with severe clinical systemic RV dysfunction, severe RV outflow tract obstruction, or recurrent or uncontrolled atrial or ventricular arrhythmias should be restricted from all competitive sports, with the possible exception of low-intensity (class IA) sports (Class III; Level of Evidence C)."

No sports, with the possible exception of Class IA sports.

Reference: Eligibility and Disqualification Recommendations for Competitive Athletes With Cardiovascular Abnormalities. A Scientific Statement From the AHA and ACC. Circulation 2015
