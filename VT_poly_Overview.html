Non-reversible or unidentified cause

"Athletes who have survived a cardiac arrest caused by ventricular fibrillation or VT or who have had documented symptomatic rapid VT associated with a defined nonreversible cardiac abnormality (structural or molecular) or unidentified cause should have an ICD placed.

See “Athletes With ICDs” for recommendations regarding competitive sports participation after ICD implantation (Class I; Level of Evidence A)."

Should have ICD placed

See also: ICD sports participation guidelines

Reversible abnormality

"Class IIb athletes who have survived a cardiac arrest caused by ventricular fibrillation or VT or who have had documented symptomatic rapid VT associated with a defined reversible abnormality (eg, resolved acute myocarditis or a controllable electrolyte abnormality) may be considered for reinstitution of participation after reevaluation at 3 months (Class I; Level of Evidence C)."

May be considered for reinstitution of participation after reevaluation at 3 months
